Nasi Uduk

Description

Nasi uduk is a dish made from white rice steamed with coconut milk and seasoned with nutmeg, cinnamon, ginger, lemongrass leaves and pepper. This popular Betawi dish is a cross between two Malay and Javanese cultures

Nasi uduk is usually served with fried chips, fried tofu, scrambled eggs or sliced fried eggs, dried shredded meat, tempeh, fried onions, fried chicken, cucumber and peanut sauce.

Ingredients

  • 500 g fluffy rice, wash, drain
  • 500 ml thin coconut milk
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 lemongrass, bruised
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 lime leaves
  • 2 beef/chicken sausages, sliced crosswise

Steps

  1. Put the rice in the rice cooker.

  2. Pour in coconut milk, add sausage pieces, ground turmeric, bay leaves, lime leaves, lemongrass and salt. Stir well.

  3. Open the rice cooker, stir briefly. Leave it in the rice cooker in the warm position.

  4. Serve warm with sausage and scrambled eggs.
